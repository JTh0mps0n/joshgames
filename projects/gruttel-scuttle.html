Overview

Gruttel Scuttle is my 2nd team project with a large quantity of people from various disciplines in university and can be played here!

Development spanned around 9 months starting during september 2021 ending on may 2022.

I was in charge of the back-end game systems of this project; developing the game-loop, "litter throwing" system into the level and "region" system which tells the AI where to go. I also assisted 2 of our other programmers with AI and other systems.

My Involvement

During the course of development, I was assigned to work on the more "back-end" features of the project. One of which being the game-loop (which ties in various other features I developed.) The game-loop was made using a behaviour tree system and this allowed us to more easily make and attach features to this system as we progressed through development.

Another feature I was assigned to was the "litter throwing" mechanic which made use of an algorithm called "Ballistic Trajectory" - This makes use of trigonometry to calculate the angle to launch a physics object based on speed and mass in order to land the object at a specific world coordinate. This system was used with a "region" system I developed which allows designers to easily select whether this is a "litter" or "home" region. This system was used with the AI programmer as it tells the Gruttels where to go.

I was also assigned to various other features such as creating UI functionality and the Gruttel selection system at the start of the game.
